Cheat 1 : The Shapeshifting datalist: Autocomplete Your Way to Glory

Ever dream of creating a custom autocomplete dropdown without JavaScript? Well, dream no more! The datalist attribute is your new best friend. Just add it to your input element, define a list of options within the same tag, and voila! Instant magic.

Example

<input type="text" list="cities" placeholder="Type or Select City"> <details="text" list="cities"> <datalist id="cities"> <option value="Paris"> <option value="Tokyo"> <option value="New York"> </datalist>

Cheat 2: The Collapsible Content: Expandable Sections with details:

Create interactive, collapsible sections for cleaner website layouts with the details and summary elements. Users can click the summary to reveal or hide the detailed content.

Example

Click to see our latest news!

Exciting new features are coming soon!

More updates are coming soon!

<details> <summary>Click to see our latest news!</summary> <p>Exciting new features are coming soon!</p> <p>More updates are coming soon!</p> </datails>

Cheat 3: The Supercharged optgroup: Organize Dropdowns Like a Pro

Remember that friend who always has the perfectly organized bookshelf, making finding the right book a breeze? That's the power you hold with the optgroup tag in HTML! It lets you group related options within your dropdown lists (select element), making them easier to navigate and scan for users.

Example

<label for="framework"> Select Framework: </label> <select id="framework"> <optgroup label="Frontend"> <option>React</option> <option>Angular</option> <option>Vue.js</option> </optgroup> </select> <optgroup label="Backend"> <option>Django</option> <option>Laravel</option> <option>Express.js</option> </optgroup> </select>

Cheat 4: Superscript and Subscript: The Underdog Formatting Heroes

We all know about bold and italics, the formatting superstars of the HTML world. But there are two lesser-known heroes lurking in the shadows: subscript (sub) and superscript (sup) tags. These guys might not be the flashiest, but they pack a powerful punch when it comes to adding precision and clarity to your content.

Think chemical formulas (H₂O), mathematical equations (a³ + b³ = (a + b)³), or even footnotes (¹see reference at the bottom). This is where these tags truly shine.

Example

5 2 = 25

Water = H2O

<p> 5<sup>2</sup> = 25 </p> <p> Water=H<sub>2<sub>O </p>

Cheat 5: Beyond the Bar: Mastering the Meter Tag for Visually Engaging Progress Displays

Progress bars. We've all seen them, those rectangular strips filling up to indicate completion. But what if you could ditch the mundane and transform your progress displays into something truly engaging and dynamic? Enter the meter tag, your secret weapon for crafting visually captivating progress indicators.

Forget static images or generic bars. The meter tag lets you define a visual representation of progress within a specified range. Imagine showing download progress as a filling water bottle, a quiz completion as a color-changing bar, or even a workout progress tracker as a glowing ring.

Example

75%
<meter value="75" min="0" max="100">75%</meter>

Cheat 6: The Unobtrusive abbr:

Ever seen an acronym or abbreviation with a dotted underline? That's the work of the abbr tag. It displays abbreviations and provides a full explanation when hovered over using the title attribute, making your content accessible to everyone.

Example

WWW
<abbr title="World Wide Web">WWW</abbr>

Cheat 7: Highlighting text with mark:

The mark tag highlights a section of text like a marker would on paper. It's useful for drawing attention to important parts of your content.

Example

Remember to double-check your code before deploying.

<p>Remember to<mark>double-check</mark> your code before deploying.</p>
